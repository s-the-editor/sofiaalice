Saga of the Necromancer

Saga of the Necromancer is a high fantasy novel in its first draft. It currently sits at about 50k words.

The story follows Lenora, an infamous necromancer, from childhood to adulthood. As a child, she discovers her powers in the wastes of Nyvaris. As a young adult, she travels to Zephria to attend the School of Magecraft at the University of Aurelith. She becomes a celebrated wizard and joins the newly-created Wardens of Aurelith to protect her new country from rogue sorcery. Though she finds love and friendship in the Wardens, she slowly realizes that they don't fight for goodness, but the interests of a charismatic but ill-intentioned king.

The novel explores themes of how the narrative surrounding mythic figures is controlled by the dominant culture; the necessity of love, friendship, and relationships in adult fantasy; and the importance of diversity in scholarship. Above all, it's a story about magic, strong women, and adventure.



Ghost Hunt

Ghost Hunt is a supernatural literary novella just under 20k words. It was submitted as an honors thesis to Emory University's English and Creative Writing program, where it received High Honors.

The story follows Jillian, a psychic haunted by visions of a future she cannot change, and a group of five teenaged former ghost hunters. Riley, Freddy, and Greene all live in Oxborough, a rural Maine town. Riley convinces the other two to search for a missing child in an attempt to keep the friend group together. Laurie and Sarah, sisters, used to vacation in Oxborough over the summer. Ever since Laurie’s boyfriend died in a car accident in Oxborough, neither she nor Sarah have returned. Sarah, obsessed with Laurie’s accident, runs away to Oxborough to figure out what really happened. This novel explores how relationships fall apart at the end of high school, the intricacies of belonging and identity, and how the supernatural overlaps with the mundane.

Currently, portions of this piece are submitted for publication in literary journals.
